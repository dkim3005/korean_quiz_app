1. Irregular Verbs (불규칙 동사)

Some verbs and adjectives change their stem in unexpected ways when a vowel follows. Set 2 covers several types.

'ㅂ' Irregular

The final consonant ㅂ of the stem changes to 우 when followed by a vowel. A special exception is 돕다 (to help), where ㅂ changes to 오.

춥다 (cold) + 어요 → 추우 + 어요 → 추워요

돕다 (help) + 아요 → 도오 + 아요 → 도와요

'ㄷ' Irregular

The final consonant ㄷ of the stem changes to ㄹ when followed by a vowel. (Note: Not all 'ㄷ' verbs are irregular, e.g., 닫다 → 닫아요).

듣다 (listen) + 어요 → 들 + 어요 → 들어요

걷다 (walk) + 어요 → 걸 + 어요 → 걸어요

'ㅅ' Irregular

The final consonant ㅅ of the stem is dropped when followed by a vowel.

짓다 (build) + 어요 → 지 + 어요 → 지어요

'르' Irregular

When a stem ending in 르 is followed by -아/어요, the 으 is dropped and an extra ㄹ is added to the syllable before it.

모르다 (not know) + 아요 → 몰 + ㄹ + 아요 → 몰라요

'ㅎ' Irregular (Adjectives)

For some adjectives ending in ㅎ, the ㅎ is dropped and the vowel changes when -아요/어요 is added.

하얗다 (white) + 아요 → 하얘요

빨갛다 (red) + 아요 → 빨개요

'ㄹ' Regular Conjugation Note

Verbs with stems ending in `ㄹ` (like `만들다`) are regular with past tense `-았/었어요`. The `ㄹ` does not drop.

만들다 (to make) + 었어요 → 만들었어요

2. Honorifics (높임말)

Used to show respect to the person you are talking about (usually someone older or of higher social status).

Honorific Particle & Verb Ending

  • Particle -께서: The honorific version of the subject particles 이/가.
  • Verb Infix -(으)시-: Added to the verb stem to make it honorific.

선생님이 읽어요. (Standard)

선생님께서 책을 읽으세요. (Honorific)

'께서' replaces '이', and '읽다' becomes '읽으시다' → '읽으세요'.

Special Honorific Verbs

Some verbs have special honorific forms that must be memorized.

  • 먹다 (to eat) / 마시다 (to drink) → 드시다 or 잡수시다
  • 자다 (to sleep) → 주무시다
  • 있다 (to be/have) → 계시다

아버지가 밥을 먹어요. (Standard)

아버지께서 진지를 드세요. (Honorific)

(Note: The word for 'meal' also changes from `밥` to `진지` in this context.)

3. Particles (조사)

에서: For Action Location and Origin

에서 has two key meanings:

  • Location of an action: "at", "in" (e.g., I study AT the library.)
  • Point of origin: "from" (e.g., I came FROM China.)

학교에서 한국어를 배웠어요. (Action Location)

I learned Korean at school.

중국에서 왔어요. (Origin)

I came from China.

4. Connecting Clauses (문장 연결)

-고 vs. -지만

  • -고 (and / and then): Simply connects two clauses in sequence.
  • -지만 (but): Connects two contrasting clauses.

백화점에 가고 옷을 샀어요.

I went to the department store and then bought clothes.

어렵지만 재미있어요.

It's difficult, but it's interesting.

-(으)니까 vs. -아서/어서 (Reason)

Both mean "because", but have a key difference.

  • -아서/어서: General reason. Cannot be used with suggestions (-을까요?) or commands (-으세요).
  • -(으)니까: Can be a more subjective reason. Can be used with suggestions and commands.

배고프니까 밥 먹을까요?

I'm hungry, so shall we eat?

배고파서 밥 먹을까요? (Incorrect)

그래서 (So / Therefore)

A standalone conjunction that links two separate sentences, showing a cause-and-effect relationship.

어제 잠을 못 잤어요. 그래서 오늘 피곤해요.

I couldn't sleep yesterday. Therefore, I am tired today.

5. Expressing Desire, Ability, and Opinions

Desire: -고 싶다 / -고 싶어하다

  • -고 싶어요: Expresses your own (1st person) desire. "I want to...".
  • -고 싶어해요: Describes someone else's (3rd person) desire. "He/She wants to...".

저는 한국에 가고 싶어요.

I want to go to Korea.

Ability: 못 (Can't) vs. 안 (Don't)

  • 안: Simple negation or unwillingness. "I don't eat."
  • 못: Expresses inability. "I can't eat (because it's spicy, I'm sick, etc.)."

매운 음식을 못 먹어요.

I can't eat spicy food.

Opinion/Suggestion: N + 은/는 어때요?

Asks for an opinion about a noun, often as a suggestion. "How about...?"

공원은 어때요?

How about the park?

6. Negation (부정문)

Negating Nouns: N + 이/가 아니다

To say "is not Noun", you use 아니다 with the subject particle 이/가.

저는 학생이 아니에요.

I am not a student.

7. Numbers & Counters (숫자와 단위 명사)

Native Korean Numbers + Counters

Remember that numbers 1-4 change before a counter word (한, 두, 세, 네).

  • 병: counter for bottles.
  • 명: counter for people.
  • 벌: counter for clothes.

물 두 병 (Two bottles of water)

사람 다섯 명 (Five people)

티셔츠 세 벌 (Three t-shirts)

8. Key Vocabulary & Phrases

  • 제 vs. 저의: Both mean "my". 제 is the common contraction of 저의.
  • 덜: Adverb meaning "less". (e.g., 덜 맵게 해주세요 - Please make it less spicy.)
  • 같이: Adverb meaning "together". (e.g., 같이 계산할게요 - We'll pay together.)
  • 지난: Adjective meaning "last" or "past". (e.g., 지난 주말 - last weekend).
  • 늦게: Adverb meaning "late". (e.g., 늦게 일어났어요 - I woke up late).
  • 약속이 있다: A set phrase meaning "to have plans/an appointment".
  • 낮잠을 자다: A set phrase meaning "to take a nap".
  • 실례합니다: "Excuse me." Used to get attention or apologize for a minor interruption.
  • Places: 백화점 (department store), 수영장 (swimming pool).
  • Weather: 맑다 (to be clear/sunny), 흐리다 (to be cloudy).
