Learning Objectives

  • Introduce your name and nationality.
  • Ask for someone's name and nationality.
  • Use the topic marking particles 은/는.
  • Use the verb 'to be' in the polite form, 이에요/예요.

Core Grammar

1. Noun + 은/는 (Topic Marker)

This particle attaches to a noun to indicate that it is the main topic of the sentence. It's similar to "as for..." or "speaking of..." in English.

Rule:

  • Use 은 after a noun ending in a **consonant**. (e.g., 학생 + 은 → 학생은)
  • Use 는 after a noun ending in a **vowel**. (e.g., 저 + 는 → 저는)

Examples:

저는 마이클입니다. (I am Michael.)

이것은 책입니다. (This is a book.)

2. Noun + 이에요/예요 (to be)

This is the polite, informal way to say "to be" (am, are, is). It's used to state what something or someone is.

Rule:

  • Use 이에요 after a noun ending in a **consonant**. (e.g., 학생 + 이에요 → 학생이에요)
  • Use 예요 after a noun ending in a **vowel**. (e.g., 의사 + 예요 → 의사예요)

Examples:

저는 미국 사람이에요. (I am an American.)

저는 유나예요. (I am Yuna.)

Extra Tip!

When asking a question, simply raise the intonation at the end of the sentence. (e.g., 학생이에요?)
