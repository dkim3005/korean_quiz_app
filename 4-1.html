1. Comprehensive Honorifics (높임말 종합)

Honorifics are essential for polite Korean. They involve special nouns, verbs, and particles to show respect.

Special Honorific Verbs

These verbs must be memorized as they replace common verbs.

  • 자다 (to sleep) → 주무시다
  • 있다 (to be/exist) → 계시다 (Negative: 안 계시다)
  • 아프다 (to be sick) → 편찮으시다
  • 주다 (to give) → 드리다 (This is a humble form, used when you give to an elder)

어머니께서 주무세요. (My mother is sleeping.)

할머니께서 편찮으세요. (My grandmother is sick.)

Special Honorific Nouns

Common nouns also have respectful equivalents.

  • 집 (house) → 댁
  • 이름 (name) → 성함
  • 나이 (age) → 연세
  • 생일 (birthday) → 생신

아버지 성함은 어떻게 되세요? (What is your father's name?)

할아버지, 생신 축하드려요. (Grandfather, happy birthday.)

Honorific Particles

  • 께서: Replaces subject particles 이/가.
  • 께: Replaces destination particles 에게/한테 (to someone).

교수님께서 학교에 안 계세요.

The professor is not at school.

할머니께 선물을 드렸어요.

I gave a gift to my grandmother.

2. Expressing Guesses & Speculation (추측)

-(으)ㄴ/는/(으)ㄹ 것 같다 (It seems like...)

Used to express a guess or a softened opinion. The tense changes the preceding modifier.

  • Future: Adjective/Verb + -(으)ㄹ 것 같다 → 비가 올 것 같아요. (It seems like it will rain.)
  • Present: Adjective/Verb + -(으)ㄴ/는 것 같다 → 비가 오는 것 같아요. (It seems like it's raining.)
  • Past: Adjective/Verb + -(으)ㄴ 것 같다 → 비가 온 것 같아요. (It seems like it rained.)
  • With Nouns: Noun + 인 것 같다 or simply Noun + 같다 → 저 사람은 학생인 것 같아요. / 학생같아요. (He seems like a student.)

-겠- (It must be... / It looks...)

Expresses a strong assumption or guess based on direct evidence or observation.

음식이 맛있겠어요. (Seeing the food)

The food looks delicious. / That must be delicious.

네가 다 해서 정말 피곤하겠어요. (Seeing your friend worked hard)

You did everything, you must be so tired.

3. Expressing Ability & Inability (능력/불능력)

안 (Don't) vs. 못 (Can't)

A crucial distinction: 안 is about will, 못 is about ability.

  • 안: Negation of will or choice. (e.g., 저는 아침을 안 먹어요. - I don't eat breakfast.)
  • 못: Negation of ability or possibility due to external factors. (e.g., 바빠서 파티에 못 가요. - I can't go to the party because I'm busy.)

-(으)ㄹ 수 있다/없다 (Can / Cannot)

A more formal or explicit way to state ability/inability.

한국어를 조금 할 수 있어요.

I can speak a little Korean.

결정할 수 없어요. = 결정 못 해요.

I can't decide.

4. Prohibition & Permission (금지/허가)

Prohibition: -지 마세요 & -(으)면 안 되다

  • -지 마세요: A direct negative command. "Please don't...".
  • -(으)면 안 되다: States that something is not allowed or one must not do it. "You shouldn't..." / "It's not okay if you...".

여기에서 담배를 피우지 마세요.

Please don't smoke here.

여기에서 담배를 피우면 안 돼요.

You must not smoke here.

Permission: -아/어도 되다/괜찮다

Means "it's okay to..." or "you may...". When used with a negative verb, it means "you don't have to...".

내일 안 와도 돼요. = 내일 오지 않아도 돼요.

You don't have to come tomorrow. / It's okay if you don't come.

5. Expressing Experience & Trying (경험/시도)

-아/어 보다

This grammar has two key meanings depending on the context:

  • Experience (Past Tense): To have the experience of having done something.
  • Trying (Present/Future Tense): To try doing something.

제주도에 가 봤어요. (Experience)

I have been to Jeju Island.

이 옷을 한번 입어 볼게요. (Trying)

I will try on these clothes.

6. Expressing Time Sequence (시간 순서)

Before & After

  • Before: N + 전에 / V-기 전에
  • After: N + 후에 / V-(으)ㄴ 후에

Note the different verb endings required: -기 for 'before' and -(으)ㄴ for 'after'.

밥을 먹기 전에 손을 씻어요.

I wash my hands before eating.

수업이 끝난 후에 영화를 볼 거예요.

I will watch a movie after class ends.

7. Grammar Nuances (문법 뉘앙스)

-(으)ㄹ게요 vs. -(으)ㄹ 거예요 (Future Tense)

  • -(으)ㄹ 거예요: A neutral statement about a future plan or fact.
  • -(으)ㄹ게요: A promise or intention made in response to the listener or the current situation. It shows the speaker's action is relevant to the listener.

A: 추워요. (It's cold.)

B: 제가 창문을 닫을게요. (I'll close the window.)

(B's action is a direct response to A's statement.)

8. Key Phrases & Vocabulary (주요 표현 및 어휘)

  • 그냥: just, simply (e.g., 그냥 쉬었어요 - I just rested.)
  • 더: more (e.g., 더 큰 거 - a bigger one)
  • 무슨 / 어떤: what kind of, which (often interchangeable)
  • 늦어서 죄송합니다: I'm sorry for being late.
  • 다시 한번 말씀해 주세요: Please say it again.
  • 얼마나 걸려요?: How long does it take?
  • 쯤: about, approximately (e.g., 30분쯤 - about 30 minutes)
  • 까지 / (으)로: (to / toward) for destinations. (e.g., 시청까지 가요. / 시청으로 가요.)
