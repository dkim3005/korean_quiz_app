Detailed Grammar Points

1. Formal Verb Ending: Verb + -습니다/ㅂ니다

This is a formal, polite verb ending. It's more formal than the '-아요/어요' ending and is often used in public announcements, news reports, business presentations, and the military. To use it, you first find the verb stem by dropping '-다'.

Rule:

  • After a verb stem ending in a **consonant**, add -습니다.
  • After a verb stem ending in a **vowel**, add -ㅂ니다 by attaching 'ㅂ' as the new final consonant.

Examples:

가다 (to go) → 가 + ㅂ니다 → 갑니다. (I go.)

먹다 (to eat) → 먹 + 습니다 → 먹습니다. (I eat.)

일하다 (to work) → 일하 + ㅂ니다 → 일합니다. (I work.)

2. Object Particle: Noun + 을/를

This particle marks the direct object of a sentence—the noun that receives the action of the verb.

Rule:

  • After a noun ending in a **consonant**, use 을.
  • After a noun ending in a **vowel**, use 를.

Examples:

저는 밥을 먹습니다. (I eat a meal.) - '밥' ends in a consonant.

유나 씨는 커피를 마십니다. (Yuna drinks coffee.) - '커피' ends in a vowel.

학생은 한국어를 공부합니다. (The student studies Korean.) - '한국어' ends in a vowel.

3. Location Particles: Noun + 에 vs. 에서

These particles indicate location, but they are not interchangeable. Their usage depends on the verb.

Key Distinction:

  • 에 (static location / destination): Used with verbs of movement like 가다 (go), 오다 (come), 다니다 (attend) to indicate the destination. It is also used with 있다 (to be at) / 없다 (to not be at) to show where something is.
  • 에서 (action location): Used with action verbs (e.g., 먹다, 공부하다, 일하다) to show where the action happens.

Examples:

저는 회사에 갑니다. (I go to the company.) - Destination of '가다'.

저는 회사에서 일합니다. (I work at the company.) - Location of the action '일하다'.

친구가 집에 있습니다. (My friend is at home.) - Static location of '있다'.

학생이 도서관에서 공부합니다. (The student studies at the library.) - Location of the action '공부하다'.
