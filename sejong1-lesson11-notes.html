Key Expressions & Grammar

1. Asking "Why?": 왜

'왜' is the question word for "why". It's used to ask for the reason behind an action or state.

Example Question:

왜 한국어를 공부해요?

(Why do you study Korean?)

2. Giving Reasons: -아서/어서

To answer a "why" question, you can connect the reason and the result with the '-(아/어)서' grammar. It means "because" or "so".

Rule:

  • If the verb/adjective stem's last vowel is ㅏ or ㅗ, add -아서.
  • If the verb/adjective stem's last vowel is anything else, add -어서.
  • For 하다 verbs, it becomes 해서.

Examples:

저는 한국 영화를 좋아해서 한국어를 공부해요.

(Because I like Korean movies, I study Korean.)

한국 친구가 있어서 한국어를 공부해요.

(Because I have a Korean friend, I study Korean.)

3. Asking for an Opinion: -이/가 어때요?

To ask how someone feels about something, you can use '어때요?'. This is often used to ask about their experience with studying Korean.

Example Question & Answers:

Q: 한국어 공부가 어때요?

(How is studying Korean?)

A1: 아주 재미있어요. (It's very fun.)

A2: 조금 어려워요. (It's a little difficult.)

A3: 괜찮아요. (It's okay.)
