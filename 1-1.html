1. Basic Sentence Structure (A is B)

The most basic sentence pattern in Korean is "Noun A is Noun B." This is expressed using 이에요/예요.

Noun + 이에요/예요

This is the "to be" verb in the standard polite form.

  • 이에요 is used after a noun ending in a consonant.
  • 예요 is used after a noun ending in a vowel.

이것은 책이에요. (책 ends with a consonant 'ㄱ')

This is a book.

저는 의사예요. (의사 ends with a vowel 'ㅏ')

I am a doctor.

2. Essential Particles (조사)

Particles are attached to nouns to show their role in the sentence.

Topic Particle: 은/는

Marks the main topic of the sentence ("As for A..."). It introduces what you are talking about.

  • 은 follows a noun ending in a consonant.
  • 는 follows a noun ending in a vowel.

이것은 책이에요. (이것 ends in a consonant 'ㅅ')

As for this, it's a book.

저는 학생이에요. (저 ends in a vowel 'ㅓ')

As for me, I am a student.

Object Particle: 을/를

Marks the direct object—the noun that receives the verb's action.

  • 을 follows a noun ending in a consonant.
  • 를 follows a noun ending in a vowel.

빵을 먹어요. (빵 ends in a consonant 'ㅇ')

I eat bread.

사과를 먹어요. (사과 ends in a vowel 'ㅘ')

I eat an apple.

Location/Time Particles: 에 vs. 에서

These two particles both relate to location, but have a critical difference.

  • 에: Marks a destination (going TO), a static location (BEING at), or a specific time (AT 3pm).
  • 에서: Marks the location where an action happens (DOING something AT).

9시에 회사에 가요.

I go to the company at 9 o'clock. (Time & Destination)

도서관에서 공부해요.

I study at the library. (Action of studying happens here)

Tool/Method Particle: (으)로

Indicates the tool, means, or method used to do something.

  • 로 follows a vowel or the consonant 'ㄹ'.
  • 으로 follows a consonant (except 'ㄹ').

버스로 가요.

I go by bus.

컴퓨터로 공부해요.

I study with/by computer.

3. Verb Tenses (Past & Future)

Past Tense: -았/었어요

This is attached to a verb/adjective stem to make it past tense.

  • -았어요: Use if the last vowel of the stem is ㅏ or ㅗ.
  • -었어요: Use for all other vowels.
  • -했어요: Used for verbs ending in 하다.

보다 (to see) -> 보 + 았어요 -> 봤어요 (Contracted form)

읽다 (to read) -> 읽 + 었어요 -> 읽었어요

공부하다 (to study) -> 공부했어요

Future Tense: -(으)ㄹ 거예요

Expresses a future plan or intention ("am going to..." or "will...").

  • -ㄹ 거예요: Use after a stem ending in a vowel.
  • -을 거예요: Use after a stem ending in a consonant.

가다 (to go) -> 가 + ㄹ 거예요 -> 갈 거예요

I am going to go.

먹다 (to eat) -> 먹 + 을 거예요 -> 먹을 거예요

I am going to eat.

4. Negation & Suggestion

Negation: 안 + Verb

The simplest way to make a negative sentence. It means "don't" or "not". Simply place 안 before the verb.

가요. (I go.) -> 안 가요. (I don't go.)

먹어요. (I eat.) -> 안 먹어요. (I don't eat.)

Suggestion: -(으)ㄹ까요?

Used to make a suggestion ("Shall we...?") or ask for the listener's opinion.

내일 만날까요?

Shall we meet tomorrow?

A: "내일 만날까요?"   B: "네, 좋아요."

A: "Shall we meet tomorrow?" B: "Yes, that's good."

Reason/Result: -(아/어)서

Connects two clauses, where the first is the reason for the second ("A, so B").

아파서 병원에 갔어요.

I was sick, so I went to the hospital.

5. Irregular Verbs ('ㅂ' and 'ㄷ')

'ㅂ' Irregular

When a verb stem ends in ㅂ and is followed by a vowel, the ㅂ changes to 우.

어렵다 (difficult) + -어요 -> 어려우 + 어요 -> 어려워요

춥다 (cold) + -어요 -> 추우 + 어요 -> 추워요

'ㄷ' Irregular

When a verb stem ends in ㄷ and is followed by a vowel, the ㄷ changes to ㄹ.

듣다 (to listen) + -어요 -> 들 + 어요 -> 들어요

듣다 + -(으)면서 -> 들으면서 (while listening)

6. Numbers & Counters

Native Korean Numbers + Counters

When counting items, use Native Korean Numbers. The numbers 1-4 change form before a counter word.

  • 하나 (1) -> 한
  • 둘 (2) -> 두
  • 셋 (3) -> 세
  • 넷 (4) -> 네

잔 is the counter for drinks in cups or glasses.

커피 한 잔

One cup of coffee.

7. Question Words (의문사)

  • 뭐: What (e.g., 이거 뭐예요? - What is this?)
  • 어디: Where (e.g., 어디에 가요? - Where are you going?)
  • 언제: When (e.g., 언제 만날까요? - When shall we meet?)
  • 왜: Why (e.g., 왜 안 왔어요? - Why didn't you come?)
  • 어때요: How is it? / What do you think? (e.g., 한국어 공부가 어때요? - How is studying Korean?)

8. Politeness Levels

Standard Polite (해요체) vs. Formal (하십시오체)

Korean has different speech levels depending on formality.

  • Standard Polite -아요/어요: Used in most daily conversations. It's polite but friendly.
  • Formal -ㅂ/습니다: Used in very formal settings like news broadcasts, business presentations, or speaking to a large audience. It is more formal and impersonal.

가다 (to go) -> 가요 (Standard) / 갑니다 (Formal)

먹다 (to eat) -> 먹어요 (Standard) / 먹습니다 (Formal)

9. Key Vocabulary & Phrases

Nouns

  • 회사원: office worker
  • 집: house, home
  • 식당: restaurant
  • 물: water
  • 회의: meeting
  • 고궁: old palace

Verbs & Adjectives

  • 운동하다: to exercise
  • 자다: to sleep
  • 읽다: to read
  • 보다: to see/watch
  • 사진을 찍다: to take a picture
  • 추워요 / 더워요: it's cold / it's hot
  • 바빠요: to be busy
  • 재미있어요: to be fun/interesting

Location Words

  • 위: on top of
  • 밑/아래: under, below
  • 옆: next to, beside
  • 안: inside

Time Words

  • 어제: yesterday
  • 오늘: today
  • 내일: tomorrow
  • 지금: now
  • 올해: this year
  • 봄/여름/가을/겨울: Spring/Summer/Fall/Winter
