Detailed Grammar Points

1. Past Tense: -았/었/했어요

To talk about things that have already happened, you need to change verbs to the past tense. This is the most common way to form the past tense in polite conversation.

Rule:

  • If the verb stem's last vowel is ㅏ or ㅗ, add 았어요.
  • If the verb stem's last vowel is anything else, add 었어요.
  • For verbs ending in 하다, it becomes 했어요.

Examples:

보다 (to see) → 보 + 았어요 → 봤어요. (I saw/watched)

먹다 (to eat) → 먹 + 었어요 → 먹었어요. (I ate)

마시다 (to drink) → 마시 + 었어요 → 마셨어요. (I drank)

공부하다 (to study) → 공부했어요. (I studied)

2. Location of Action: Noun + 에서

The particle '에서' is attached to a place noun to show where an **action** takes place. It answers the question "Where did [the action] happen?".

Examples:

극장에서 영화를 봤어요. (I watched a movie at the theater.)

집에서 공부했어요. (I studied at home.)

친구를 커피숍에서 만났어요. (I met my friend at the coffee shop.)

Important: 에서 vs. 에

Use 에서 for the location of an ACTION.
Use 에 for the location of EXISTENCE (used with 있다/없다) or a destination (used with 가다/오다).
Action: 집에서 일했어요. (I worked at home.)
Existence: 집에 있었어요. (I was at home.)

3. Contraction: 무엇을 → 뭐 / 뭘

In conversation, the object "무엇을" (what) is almost always shortened for easier pronunciation.

Example:

Formal: 어제 무엇을 했어요?

Common: 어제 뭘 했어요?

Most Common: 어제 뭐 했어요? (What did you do yesterday?)
