Detailed Grammar Points

1. Future Tense: Verb + -(으)ㄹ 거예요

This grammar point is used to express a future plan, intention, or a strong probability. It's the equivalent of "will" or "going to" in English.

Rule:

  • If the verb stem ends in a **vowel** or the consonant **'ㄹ'**, add -ㄹ 거예요.
  • If the verb stem ends in a **consonant** (other than 'ㄹ'), add -을 거예요.

Examples:

가다 (to go) → 가 + ㄹ 거예요 → 주말에 고궁에 갈 거예요. (I'm going to go to the old palace on the weekend.)

읽다 (to read) → 읽 + 을 거예요 → 집에서 책을 읽을 거예요. (I'm going to read a book at home.)

쉬다 (to rest) → 쉬 + ㄹ 거예요 → 약속이 없어요. 그래서 쉴 거예요. (I have no plans. So I'm going to rest.)

2. Connecting Reason & Result: 그래서

'그래서' is a conjunction that means "so", "therefore", or "that's why". It connects two sentences where the first sentence is the reason or cause for the second sentence (the result).

Structure: [Sentence 1: Reason]. 그래서 [Sentence 2: Result].

고향에서 친구가 왔어요. 그래서 같이 박물관에 갔어요.

(A friend came from my hometown. So, we went to the museum together.)

많이 아팠어요. 그래서 병원에 갔어요.

(I was very sick. Therefore, I went to the hospital.)

Asking About Plans

To ask about past and future plans, the key is the verb ending:

Past: 주말에 뭐 했어요? (What did you do last weekend?)

Future: 주말에 뭐 할 거예요? (What are you going to do this weekend?)
