Counting Units (Counters / 계수사)

In Korean, you must use a specific "counter" word after a number when counting things. It's like saying "one bottle of water" or "two cups of coffee."

개 (gae): for general items (most common counter)

Example: 사과 세 개 (three apples), 빵 한 개 (one piece of bread)

명 (myeong): for people

Example: 학생 두 명 (two students), 친구 네 명 (four friends)

병 (byeong): for bottles

Example: 물 한 병 (one bottle of water), 맥주 다섯 병 (five bottles of beer)

잔 (jan): for cups/glasses

Example: 커피 한 잔 (one cup of coffee), 우유 두 잔 (two glasses of milk)

권 (gwon): for books

Example: 책 세 권 (three books)

Detailed Grammar Points

1. Number Form Change (1, 2, 3, 4)

When the native Korean numbers for 1, 2, 3, and 4 come before a counter, they change their form by dropping the last letter.

Rule:

  • 하나 (one) → 한
  • 둘 (two) → 두
  • 셋 (three) → 세
  • 넷 (four) → 네
  • Numbers from five (다섯) onwards do not change.

Examples:

사과 한 개 주세요. (Please give me one apple.)

물 두 병 주세요. (Please give me two bottles of water.)

학생 네 명 있어요. (There are four students.)

2. Noun Connector: Noun + 하고

This particle connects two nouns and means "and" or "with". It is often used in conversation.

Examples:

빵하고 우유를 사요. (I buy bread and milk.)

사과하고 오렌지 주세요. (Please give me apples and oranges.)

저는 친구하고 시장에 가요. (I go to the market with my friend.)

3. Formal Endings: -(스)ㅂ니다 / -(스)ㅂ니까?

This is a formal way to end sentences, often used in public announcements, news, business, and when showing a high level of respect. It is more formal than the '-아요/어요' style.

Rule (Statements):

  • After a verb stem ending in a **consonant**, use 습니다. (e.g., 먹다 → 먹습니다)
  • After a verb stem ending in a **vowel**, use ㅂ니다. (e.g., 가다 → 갑니다)

Rule (Questions):

  • After a verb stem ending in a **consonant**, use 습니까? (e.g., 먹다 → 먹습니까?)
  • After a verb stem ending in a **vowel**, use ㅂ니까? (e.g., 가다 → 갑니까?)

Examples:

A: 우유 있습니까? (Do you have milk? - Formal Question)

B: 네, 있습니다. (Yes, we have. - Formal Statement)

A: 무엇을 합니까? (What are you doing? - Formal Question)
