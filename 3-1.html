1. Verb Tenses & Conjugation

This set heavily reviews past and future tense conjugations, including irregulars.

Future Tense: -(으)ㄹ 거예요

Used for future plans or intentions ("will" / "am going to").

  • After vowel or ㄹ stem: -ㄹ 거예요 → - 거예요 (e.g., 만나다 → 만날 거예요)
  • After consonant stem: -을 거예요 (e.g., 읽다 → 읽을 거예요)
  • For stems already ending in ㄹ (e.g., 살다, 놀다, 열다), just add 거예요. The ㄹ is not repeated.

내일 방을 청소할 거예요. (I will clean my room tomorrow.)

도서관에서 책을 읽을 거예요. (I will read a book at the library.)

내일 뭐 할 거예요? - 집에서 놀 거예요. (What will you do tomorrow? - I will hang out/play at home.)

Past Tense Conjugation Rules

Review of how different verb stems combine with -았/었어요.

  • Vowel Contraction: Vowels often combine for simpler pronunciation.
    • 주다 (to give) + 었어요 → 주 + 었어요 → 줬어요
    • 배우다 (to learn) + 었어요 → 배우 + 었어요 → 배웠어요 (우+어=워)
    • 기다리다 (to wait) + 었어요 → 기다리 + 었어요 → 기다렸어요 (이+어=여)
  • '으' Vowel Drop: The vowel 으 is dropped when adding -았/었어요. The choice between 았/었 depends on the vowel before 으.
    • 쓰다 (to write) → 썼어요 (No preceding vowel, so 었 is used)
    • 아프다 (to be sick) → 아팠어요 ('ㅏ' is before, so 았 is used)
  • Irregular Verbs: Remember their unique changes.
    • 'ㄷ' irregular: 듣다 → 들었어요, 걷다 → 걸었어요
    • '르' irregular: 모르다 → 몰랐어요, 빠르다 → 빨랐어요, 고르다 → 골랐어요
    • 'ㅎ' irregular: 파랗다 (to be blue) → 파랬어요

2. Connecting Clauses (이유, 순서, 대조)

Grammar used to link two smaller sentences (clauses) into one.

-고 (Sequence)

Means "and" or "and then". It simply lists actions in chronological order.

점심을 먹고 커피를 마셨어요.

I ate lunch and then drank coffee.

-(아/어)서 (Reason / Sequential Result)

Indicates that the first clause is the reason for or leads directly to the second clause.

피곤해서 일찍 잘 거예요.

Because I'm tired, I will sleep early.

친구를 만나서 같이 밥을 먹었어요.

I met my friend, and so (as a result) we ate together.

-지만 (Contrast)

Means "but" or "although". It connects two contrasting ideas.

피곤했지만 숙제했어요.

I was tired, but I did my homework.

3. Conjunctions (접속사)

Words that connect two full sentences.

  • 하지만 / 그런데 (But, However): Show contrast. 그런데 can also mean "by the way" and feels slightly more conversational.
  • 그래서 (So, Therefore): Shows a logical cause-and-effect relationship.
  • 그러면 (Then, In that case): Used to proceed logically from a statement or condition.

비가 와요. 하지만 우산이 없어요.

It's raining. However, I don't have an umbrella.

A: 배고파요. (I'm hungry.) B: 그러면 식당에 갈까요?

B: In that case, shall we go to a restaurant?

4. Expressing Conditions: -(으)면

-(으)면 (If / When)

This grammar sets up a condition or a time for the following clause.

비가 오면 안 갈 거예요. (Condition: If)

If it rains, I will not go.

일이 끝나면 집에 갈 거예요. (Time: When)

When work is finished, I will go home.

도착하면 전화할게요.

When I arrive, I'll call you.

5. Expressing Purpose: -(으)러 가다/오다

-(으)러 + Movement Verb

Expresses the purpose for going or coming somewhere. It means "in order to (do verb)".

돈을 찾으러 은행에 가요.

I go to the bank (in order) to withdraw money.

한국어를 공부하러 한국에 왔어요.

I came to Korea (in order) to study Korean.

6. Future Tense Negation

Two ways to say "will not do"

Both forms are correct and widely used.

  • 안 + Verb: e.g., 안 할 거예요. (Generally more common in speech)
  • Verb Stem + -지 않을 거예요: e.g., 하지 않을 거예요. (Can feel slightly more formal or emphatic)

내일 일을 안 할 거예요. = 내일 일을 하지 않을 거예요.

I will not work tomorrow.

7. Honorific Particle: 께

께 (To a respected person)

This is the honorific version of the particles 에게 and 한테, which both mean "to (a person)". You use it when the recipient is someone you must show respect to, like parents, grandparents, or teachers.

친구한테 전화해요. (To a friend)

부모님께 전화할 거예요. (To parents)

I will call my parents.
