1. Noun Modifying Endings (관형사형 어미)

This grammar turns a verb or adjective into a modifier that can describe a noun, like "the book I read" or "the person who is singing".

Action Verbs

  • Past: -(으)ㄴ → 어제 읽은 책 (The book that I read yesterday)
  • Present: -는 → 지금 노래하는 사람 (The person who is singing now)
  • Future: -(으)ㄹ → 내일 갈 장소 (The place I will go tomorrow)

2. Expressing Reason & Contrast

N 때문에 / V-기 때문에 (Because of)

Expresses a clear reason. It is often used when the result is negative.

눈 때문에 못 갔어요.

I couldn't go because of the snow.

A/V-(으)ㄴ/는데 (Background or Contrast)

A very versatile connector with two main uses:

  • Background: Provides the situation for the main clause. (Similar to "..., so...")
  • Contrast: Connects two contrasting ideas. (Similar to "..., but...")

내일 시험이 있는데 공부해야 해요. (Background)

I have an exam tomorrow, so I have to study.

친구한테 전화했는데 안 받았어요. (Contrast)

I called my friend, but they didn't answer.

3. Expressing Condition & Intention

V-(으)려면 (If you intend to V)

A conditional statement about an intention. It combines the meanings of "if" (-(으)면) and "intend to" (-(으)려고 하다).

한국어를 잘하려면 매일 공부해야 해요.

If you want to be good at Korean, you have to study every day.

A/V-아/어도 (Even if / Even though)

Shows that the result in the second clause happens regardless of the condition in the first clause.

비가 와도 갈 거예요.

Even if it rains, I'm going to go.

4. Time & Immediacy

V-자마자 (As soon as)

Indicates that the second action happens immediately after the first action finishes.

회의가 끝나자마자 전화할게요.

I'll call you as soon as the meeting ends.

V-는 길에 (On the way)

Used with movement verbs (가다, 오다) to describe an action that happens while on the way somewhere.

회사에 가는 길에 은행에 들렀어요.

I stopped by the bank on my way to work.

V-는 동안 (While / During)

Refers to the duration of time in which an action is taking place.

제가 전화하는 동안 잠깐만 기다려 주세요.

Please wait a moment while I'm on the phone.

V-(으)ㄴ 지 [time]이/가 되다 (It's been... since)

Used to express the amount of time that has passed since an action occurred.

한국에 온 지 1년이 되었어요.

It's been one year since I came to Korea.

5. Appearance, Tendency & Degree

A/V-아/어 보이다 (To look / seem)

Used to describe the outward appearance or impression of something or someone.

오늘 행복해 보여요.

You look happy today.

A/V-(으)ㄴ/는 편이다 (To tend to be...)

Used to state a general tendency rather than an absolute fact. "On the ... side".

저는 아침을 많이 먹는 편이에요.

I tend to eat a lot for breakfast.

A/V-(으)ㄹ 정도로 (To the extent that...)

Indicates the degree or extent of a state or action. "So... that...".

밥 먹을 시간도 없을 정도로 바빴어요.

I was busy to the extent that I didn't even have time to eat.

6. Alternatives & Exchanges

N 말고 (Not N, but...)

Used to reject the preceding noun and suggest an alternative.

커피 말고 주스 주세요.

Not coffee, but please give me juice.

A/V-(으)ㄴ/는 대신에 (Instead of / In return for)

Indicates that one action is done as a substitute or compensation for another.

제가 도와주는 대신에 커피를 사 주세요.

In return for me helping you, please buy me coffee.

7. "Almost" and "No Choice"

V-(으)ㄹ 뻔했다 (Almost did)

Expresses that an event nearly happened, but ultimately did not.

기차를 놓칠 뻔했어요.

I almost missed the train (but I caught it).

V-(으)ㄹ 수밖에 없다 (No choice but to...)

Indicates that there is no other option available besides doing the verb.

세일해서 살 수밖에 없었어요.

It was on sale, so I had no choice but to buy it.

8. Other Key Grammar Patterns

A/V-잖아요 (As you know...)

Used to state a reason or fact that the speaker assumes the listener already knows. It has a reminding tone.

A: 왜 이렇게 사람이 많아요? (Why are there so many people?)

B: 오늘 주말이잖아요. (It's the weekend, you know!)

A/V-군요 / -는군요 (Realization)

Similar to -네요, this expresses surprise or a new realization. It can sound slightly more formal or be more common in writing.

아, 한국어를 정말 잘하시는군요.

Oh, (I realize now) you speak Korean really well.

N에 따라 다르다 (It depends on N)

A set phrase used to state that a result will vary depending on the noun.

A: 여행 비용은 얼마나 들어요? (How much does the trip cost?)

B: 날짜에 따라 달라요. (It depends on the date.)
