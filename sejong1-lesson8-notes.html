Lesson 8: 시간과 날짜 (Time and Date)

Learn how to ask for and state the date, day of the week, and time.

Dates: Months & Days (날짜: 월/일)

Dates are expressed using Sino-Korean numbers (일, 이, 삼...). You say the month number + 월 (month), and the day number + 일 (day).

Irregular Months: June & October

Be careful! For June, 육월 becomes 유월. For October, 십월 becomes 시월. This is for easier pronunciation.

Clock Time: Hours & Minutes (시간: 시/분)

Telling time uses a mixed number system. It's one of the trickiest parts of learning Korean numbers!

  • For Hours (시), you must use Native Korean numbers (하나, 둘, 셋...). Remember that 1-4 change their form: 한 시, 두 시, 세 시, 네 시.
  • For Minutes (분), you must use Sino-Korean numbers (일, 이, 삼...).

Examples:

한 시 십 분 (1:10)

네 시 이십오 분 (4:25)

열두 시 삼십 분 (12:30)

Detailed Grammar Points

1. Time Particle '에'

The particle '에' is attached to time nouns (dates, days, clock times) to indicate **when** an action takes place. It acts like "at", "on", or "in" in English.

Examples:

금요일에 영화를 봐요. (I watch a movie on Friday.)

오후 3시에 회의가 있어요. (There is a meeting at 3 PM.)

7월 25일에 시험이 있어요. (There is an exam on July 25th.)

2. Asking About Time

There are different question words to ask about different aspects of time.

언제 (When)

Used for asking about a general time, day, or date.

Q: 언제 한국에 가요? (When are you going to Korea?)

몇 시에 (At what time)

Used for asking about a specific hour and minute.

Q: 몇 시에 만나요? (At what time should we meet?)

무슨 요일에 (On what day of the week)

Used for asking about a specific day of the week.

Q: 무슨 요일에 수업이 있어요? (On what day do you have class?)
