Detailed Grammar Points

1. Making Suggestions: Verb + -(으)ㄹ까요?

This is a key grammar point for making plans. Attach it to a verb to ask "Shall we...?" or "Should we...?" It's a soft, polite way to suggest an activity together.

Rule:

  • If the verb stem ends in a **vowel** or the consonant **'ㄹ'**, add -ㄹ까요?
  • If the verb stem ends in a **consonant** (other than 'ㄹ'), add -을까요?

Examples:

만나다 (to meet) → 만나 + ㄹ까요 → 우리 만날까요? (Shall we meet?)

먹다 (to eat) → 먹 + 을까요 → 저녁을 먹을까요? (Shall we eat dinner?)

만들다 (to make) → 만들 + 까요 → 같이 만들까요? (Shall we make it together?)

2. Asking for an Opinion: Noun + 어때요?

When you want to know someone's opinion about a specific suggestion (like a time, place, or activity), use '어때요?'. It's a very common and useful way to say "How about...?"

Examples:

A: 우리 언제 만날까요? (When shall we meet?)

B: 금요일 어때요? (How about Friday?)

A: 저녁 뭐 먹을까요? (What shall we eat for dinner?)

B: 불고기 어때요? (How about bulgogi?)

Conversation Flow: Making Plans

Step 1: Suggest an activity

수진 씨, 우리 내일 같이 쇼핑할까요?

Sujin, shall we go shopping together tomorrow?

Step 2: Decline politely & give a reason

미안해요. 내일은 일이 있어요.

I'm sorry. I have something to do tomorrow.

Step 3: Make a counter-suggestion

토요일은 어때요?

How about Saturday?

Step 4: Agree to the plan

네, 좋아요. 토요일에 만나요!

Yes, that's good. Let's meet on Saturday!
