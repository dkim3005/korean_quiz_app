Main Grammar Point

Expressing Future Plans: -(으)ㄹ 거예요 (Review)

This lesson reinforces the future tense ending '-(으)ㄹ 거예요'. This is the primary way to talk about any future plan, whether it's for this weekend or for your entire life.

Rule:

  • Verb stem ending in a **vowel** or **'ㄹ'** + -ㄹ 거예요.
  • Verb stem ending in a **consonant** + -을 거예요.

Examples:

Q: 방학에 뭐 할 거예요? (What are you going to do during vacation?)

A: 커피숍에서 아르바이트를 할 거예요. (I'm going to do a part-time job at a coffee shop.)

Q: 졸업하고 뭐 할 거예요? (What are you going to do after graduating?)

A: 한국 회사에 취직할 거예요. (I'm going to get a job at a Korean company.)

내년에 중국으로 유학을 갈 거예요. (I'm going to study abroad in China next year.)

Example Plans from the Dialogues

마크 (Mark)

방학에 아르바이트를 할 거예요. 그리고 한국에 여행 갈 거예요.

During vacation, I'll do a part-time job. And I'll go travel to Korea.

로라 (Laura)

올해 대학교를 졸업할 거예요. 그리고 취직할 거예요.

This year, I'm going to graduate from university. And I'm going to get a job.

수틴 (Sutin)

방학에 한국 가게에서 아르바이트를 할 거예요. 그래서 한국어를 열심히 공부할 거예요.

During vacation, I'll work part-time at a Korean store. So, I will study Korean diligently.
