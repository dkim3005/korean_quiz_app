Detailed Grammar Points

1. Making Negative Sentences with '안'

'안' is used to make a sentence negative. It means "not" and is placed before a verb or adjective.

Rule:

  • For most verbs/adjectives: 안 + Verb/Adjective.
  • For Noun-하다 verbs (like 공부하다): Noun + 안 + 하다.

Examples:

눈이 와요. (It's snowing.) → 눈이 안 와요. (It's not snowing.)

날씨가 더워요. (The weather is hot.) → 날씨가 안 더워요. (The weather is not hot.)

운동해요. (I exercise.) → 운동 안 해요. (I don't exercise.)

2. '그리고' (And - for sentences)

'그리고' is a conjunction used to connect two separate sentences. It works just like "And" or "And then" in English.

Examples:

날씨가 좋아요. 그리고 따뜻해요. (The weather is good. And it's warm.)

저는 봄을 좋아해요. 그리고 가을도 좋아해요. (I like spring. And I like autumn too.)

Important: 그리고 vs. 하고

Use 그리고 to connect full sentences.
Use 하고 to connect nouns. (e.g., 빵하고 우유 - bread and milk)

3. Particle '도' (Too, Also)

The particle '도' is added to a noun to mean "too" or "also". It often replaces the subject particles (이/가) or object particles (을/를).

Examples:

A: 저는 봄을 좋아해요. (I like spring.)
B: 저도 봄을 좋아해요. (I like spring, too.)

A: 여름은 더워요. (Summer is hot.)
B: 네, 그리고 비도 많이 와요. (Yes, and it rains a lot, too.)

A: 마크 씨는 학생이에요. (Mark is a student.)
B: 수진 씨도 학생이에요. (Sujin is a student, also.)
