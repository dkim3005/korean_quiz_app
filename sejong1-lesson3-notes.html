Sejong Korean 1

Lesson 3: 물건 사기 (Buying Things)

Learning Objectives

  • Learn vocabulary for items in a store.
  • Ask for prices and availability of items.
  • Use Native Korean numbers with counters (like 개).
  • Connect nouns using 하고 (and).
  • Politely request items using 주세요.

Core Grammar

1. Noun + 주세요 (Please give...)

A polite expression used to request something. It literally means "please give [Noun]".

사과 주세요. (Please give me an apple.)

물 한 개 주세요. (Please give me one water.)

2. Noun + 하고 + Noun (and)

This particle connects two nouns, similar to 'and' in English. It can be used regardless of whether the noun ends in a vowel or consonant.

사과하고 빵을 삽니다. (I buy an apple and bread.)

우유하고 물 주세요. (Please give me milk and water.)

3. Native Korean Numbers + 개 (Counter)

When counting general items, use Native Korean numbers followed by the counter '개'. Note that the numbers 1, 2, 3, and 4 change form before a counter.

  • 하나 → 한 개 (one item)
  • 둘 → 두 개 (two items)
  • 셋 → 세 개 (three items)
  • 넷 → 네 개 (four items)
  • 다섯 → 다섯 개 (five items - no change)
